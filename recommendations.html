Recommended Changes to Improve Milk Quality

AI-generated personalized recommendations based on your milk quality analysis. These suggestions are tailored to help you achieve optimal milk quality standards.

Diet Recommendations

Optimize cattle nutrition for better milk quality

AI-Generated

Increase Protein Intake

  • ✓ Add high-quality protein sources like soybean meal, groundnut cake, or cottonseed meal to feed
  • ✓ Target 18-20% crude protein in total feed ration
  • ✓ Provide 2-3 kg of protein-rich concentrate per day per animal

Enhance Mineral Supplementation

  • ✓ Provide mineral mixture containing calcium, phosphorus, and trace minerals
  • ✓ Ensure adequate salt (sodium chloride) intake for proper metabolism
  • ✓ Add 50-100g of mineral mixture per day to feed

Improve Feed Quality

  • ✓ Include high-quality green fodder (maize, sorghum, or leguminous crops)
  • ✓ Ensure feed is fresh, free from mold, and properly stored
  • ✓ Maintain 60:40 ratio of roughage to concentrate in diet

Hydration Management

  • ✓ Ensure clean, fresh water is available at all times
  • ✓ Provide 3-5 liters of water per kg of milk produced
  • ✓ Water temperature should be cool (15-20°C) for better consumption

Environmental Improvements

Create optimal conditions for milk production and storage

AI-Generated

Ideal Temperature Range

15-20°C
  • • Maintain milking area temperature between 15-20°C
  • • Store milk immediately at 4°C or below
  • • Use temperature-controlled storage facilities
  • • Monitor temperature continuously with IoT sensors

Shelter Cleanliness & Humidity

  • • Maintain 50-70% relative humidity in cattle shelter
  • • Ensure proper ventilation to prevent moisture buildup
  • • Clean cattle housing daily to prevent contamination
  • • Use dry bedding material and replace regularly

Milking Hygiene

  • • Wash udder and teats with clean water before milking
  • • Use sanitized milking equipment and containers
  • • Wear clean clothing and maintain personal hygiene
  • • Filter milk immediately after collection

Cattle Management Tips

Best practices for maintaining healthy, productive cattle

AI-Generated

Stress Reduction

  • • Provide comfortable resting areas with adequate space (at least 2.5 sq meters per animal)
  • • Avoid sudden changes in routine or environment
  • • Handle cattle gently and minimize loud noises
  • • Ensure proper social interaction with herd members
  • • Provide shade and protection from extreme weather

Vaccination

  • • Follow recommended vaccination schedule for common diseases (FMD, Brucellosis, etc.)
  • • Maintain vaccination records and consult with veterinarian
  • • Vaccinate new animals before introducing to herd
  • • Monitor for adverse reactions and report to veterinarian

Health Monitoring

  • • Conduct regular health checkups and body condition scoring
  • • Monitor milk production patterns and quality indicators
  • • Check for signs of mastitis, lameness, or other health issues
  • • Maintain health records and track trends over time
  • • Implement regular deworming and parasite control programs
